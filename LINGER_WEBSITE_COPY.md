## A Museum Built From My Listening History

This is not a playlist.  
It is not a chart.  
It is not a yearly recap.

It is a collection of memory rooms built from the songs I returned to, the moods I carried, the artists that stayed close, and the moments I almost forgot.

Each room represents one year. Together, they form a timeline of feeling.

## Museum Overview

From 2022 to 2026, the journey moves from holding everything quietly, to replaying what still echoes, to facing myself, releasing what was heavy, and finally stepping forward with the memories that remain.

**Memory echoes are playing: songs that came back across years.**

## The Rooms

**2022: Rain Archive**  
Pop, OST, and live songs gather like rain-soaked memories. Everything feels heavy, healing, and stored away.

**2023: Encore Stage**  
Live songs and nostalgic pop replay old feelings out loud, like private memories stepping under stage light.

**2024: Quiet Reconcile**  
Softer, reflective songs turn inward. The room feels quiet, selective, and gently repairing.

**2025: Neon Chamber**  
Dance-pop energy and emotional contrast light up the room. The music feels restless, bright, and ready to release.

**2026: Horizon Deck**  
Live echoes and open songs make the room feel lighter. The memories are still there, but now they can move forward.

## Why This Museum Exists

After my layoff in May 2026, I finally had space to streamline my life. In that pause, I realized how much I might forget: not just events, but the feelings, thoughts, and small details of past years.

One day, I opened QQ Music, an app I had used for years. I was only wondering whether my taste had changed. But as I looked through old songs, memories started coming back: a mood, a room, a version of myself, a feeling I thought had disappeared.

That was when the idea became clear:

**Time moves on. Details blur. But music remembers.**
